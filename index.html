Mother Teresa

Saint Teresa of Calcutta (known as Mother Teresa) was an Albanian-born Indian
Roman Catholic missionary and nun who devoted her life to helping those most in need.
She founded the Missionaries of Charity in India in 1950, and for over 45 years,
she ministered to the poor, sick, orphaned and dying

Awards given to Mother Teresa

  • Honorary citizenship of the United States (November 16, 1996),
  • Congressional Gold Medal (1994)
  • States Presidential Medal of Freedom (1985)
  • U Thant Peace Award 1994
  • The Nobel Peace Prize (1979)
  • Kennedy Prize (1971)
